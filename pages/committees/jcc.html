Joint Crisis Cabinet: Thirty Years' War

In recent times, more people have been growing concerned about religious extremism and the politicians who seem to benefit their own agenda by using it. If a German living in the first half of the 17th century heard our complaints, he would most likely scoff, then proceed to explain the hardships he went through “back in the old days”. Unlike our grandparents, who like to exaggerate the struggles they went through, the German wouldn’t need to exaggerate anything. Because during the Thirty Years’ War (1618-1648), Europe experienced the loss of 9% of its population, went through the “Little Ice Age”, and saw the light of the Enlightenment dim down. Some regions in Germany ended up with more than half of their population dead. A devastating conflict for humankind, the resulting Peace of Westphalia continues to be one of the most important events in history.

We aim to have our delegates -who will all be dukes and kings- experience the last of the European Wars of Religion to its fullest, feel the brutal realities and plot and scheme just like the absolute monarchs of the period did. We hope that both Catholic and Protestant leagues will enjoy this Joint Crisis Committee we have put together and decide which is the true way of the Lord.
